# Benchmark snapshot — 2026-07-21 (first local quiesced-box reference capture)

The first capture run per METHODOLOGY's "Recommended publishing run" on a known, dedicated machine rather than a
shared CI runner: **fork=5, the full `workMicros` sweep including the 10–100ms regime, and the first full
latency-mode sweep**, ~20.4 hours wall-clock. Every prior snapshot (2026-06-20, 2026-07-09) came from GitHub-hosted
4-vCPU runners where only orderings are trustworthy; this one is the reference for absolute magnitudes **on this
hardware** — a 2018-era 6-core laptop, so the absolutes are modest but the error bars and ratios are clean.

window; every other cell landed ≤ ~9%.

**KPipe PARALLEL / CPC UNORDERED ratio by cell:** 6.1× (0µs) · 4.8× (100µs) · 8.1× (1ms) · 6.6× (10ms) ·
19.1× (35ms) · 25.9× (50ms) · **41.3× (100ms)**. The gap widens exactly where the pitch says it does: CPC's
100-worker platform pool has an architectural ceiling of `workers / work-time` — predicted 10,000 rec/s at 10ms and
1,000 at 100ms; measured 8,696 and 968. The model keeps predicting the measurement.

**KEY_ORDERED / CPC KEY (same per-key-FIFO guarantee):** 0.83× (1ms)³ · **4.0× (10ms)** · 4.8× (35ms) ·
4.9× (50ms) · **5.4× (100ms)**.

³ **A finding, flagged loudly:** at 1ms this box measures KEY_ORDERED *behind* CPC KEY (52.9k ± 0.6% vs
64.0k ± 1.0%) — the 2026-07-09 CI capture measured the opposite (93.8k ± 0.8% vs 65.9k ± 1%, 1.42×). Both captures
have tight error bars; the difference is the machine (12 hyperthreads here vs 4 vCPUs there). The prime suspect is
the `KeyOrderedDispatcher` single `ReentrantLock` — more parallelism means more contention on the LRU/queue mutex,
and the sub-ms regime is where dispatch cost dominates simulated work. This is precisely the lock-contention
question the `KeyOrderedDispatchBenchmark` baseline was built to arbitrate. Until a striped-lock experiment settles
it, the honest public claim for KEY_ORDERED vs CPC KEY at 1ms is "box-dependent: 0.8×–1.4×"; the 10–100ms claims
(4–5.4×) reproduce on both machines and carry the weight.

## Latency mode — amortized per-record drain cost (sample mode, µs/record)

Each sample is one full 25k-record drain divided by 25,000 (`@OperationsPerInvocation`), so this measures **steady
throughput as cost**, not individual end-to-end record latency — p99 ≈ p50 by construction, so only the mean is
tabled. It is the inverse view of throughput and independently confirms the ordering:

| Runtime | 0µs | 100µs | 1ms | 10ms | 35ms | 50ms | 100ms |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| KPipe PARALLEL | 1.7 | 1.9 | 1.8 | 16.9 | 19.9 | 20.5 | 25.4 |
| CPC UNORDERED | 9.7 | 9.4 | 16.3 | 118.4 | 408.1 | 558.5 | 1,053.5 |
| Reactor Kafka | 3.1 | 12.7 | 111.6 | 1,020.6 | 3,413.3 | 4,751.4 | — DNF |
| raw (unsafe) | 1.6 | 1.7 | 1.8 | 2.2 | 3.3 | 3.9 | 5.8 |

KPipe's amortized cost stays within ~4.4× of the unsafe raw loop even at 100ms of blocking work per record; CPC's
grows to ~180× raw. A true per-record end-to-end latency distribution (enqueue→sink timestamps) remains future work —
this mode cannot see individual-record tail latency.

## Allocation — the honest tradeoff, re-measured

`gc.alloc.rate.norm` (bytes per record, throughput mode):

| Runtime | B/op @1ms | B/op @10ms |
|---|---:|---:|
| KPipe PARALLEL | 1,659 | 1,757 |
| KPipe KEY_ORDERED | 942 | 948 |
| raw (unsafe) | 430 | 469 |
| CPC UNORDERED | 34 | 35 |
| Reactor Kafka | 209 | 698 |
| Share (KIP-932) | 447 | 409 |

Consistent with the 2026-06-21 attribution profile: KPipe pays ~1.7 KB/record (a fresh virtual thread per record is
most of it) and buys the throughput lead under blocking work with it. CPC allocates 50× less and hits its pool
ceiling 41× sooner. Not free, and not hidden.

## Reactor DNF at 100ms is a result, not a gap

All five forks of every Reactor `workMicros=100000` cell failed the harness's fixed 2-minute drain budget
(`awaitProcessedMessages`, `MAX_WAIT_NANOS = 2 min`): at ~210 rec/s (its measured 50ms rate, halved again at
100ms), 25k records need ~4 minutes. The 50ms latency cells sat exactly at the wire — one avgt fork survived
(119.2s vs 120s) — which is why `reactor [avgt] @50ms` reports n=5 instead of n=25 (its score agrees with the
complete n=25 sample-mode cell, so it is corroborated, not trusted alone). Raising the budget would produce a
number; the number would be ~105 rec/s and change no conclusion. Recorded as DNF.
