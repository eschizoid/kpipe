## Throughput — `ParallelProcessingBenchmark`

Records / second, higher is better. Each cell is `Score ± error` from `Cnt=10` (5 iterations × 2 forks).

| Runtime                         |       `workMicros=0` |     `workMicros=100` |    `workMicros=1000` |
| ------------------------------- | -------------------: | -------------------: | -------------------: |
| **KPipe**                       | **542,412 ± 65,198** | **490,543 ± 66,648** | **483,061 ± 43,265** |
| **Raw `KafkaConsumer` + VT**    |     557,439 ± 51,512 |     520,859 ± 73,444 |     417,920 ± 80,770 |
| **Reactor Kafka**               |     150,081 ± 27,927 |       72,595 ± 7,344 |      **9,079 ± 164** |
| **Confluent Parallel Consumer** |      109,952 ± 8,544 |      114,362 ± 5,115 |         67,726 ± 899 |

### Observations

- **KPipe matches or beats raw Loom across the sweep.** At `workMicros=0` KPipe is within 3% of Raw (542k vs 557k). At
  `workMicros=100` it's within 6%. At `workMicros=1000` KPipe is **16% ahead** (483k vs 418k). The earlier snapshot
  (`2026-05-16.json`) showed Raw narrowly ahead at every cell; the difference between the two runs is largely Raw's drop
  at 1000µs (482k → 418k) combined with KPipe's gain (430k → 483k). Run-to-run variance on this laptop is real; see the
  variance note in `METHODOLOGY.md`.

- **The Reactor cliff reproduces.** Reactor is in the middle of the pack at `workMicros=0` (150k, ~75% of KPipe), drops
  _below_ Confluent at `workMicros=100`, and collapses to **9,079 ops/sec** at `workMicros=1000` — 7.5× slower than
  Confluent and 53× slower than KPipe at the same workload. The ±164 error band on 9,079 says this is consistently slow,
  not noise. `Flux.parallel(100)` running on `Schedulers.parallel()` (10-worker carrier pool on this hardware) does not
  deliver the concurrency the `.parallel(100)` argument suggests.

- **KPipe degrades gracefully.** 542k → 490k → 483k. About an 11% drop across a 1000× change in per-record work. Virtual
  threads scale beyond the partition count; blocked records cost kilobytes of stack and the carrier thread keeps serving
  other work.

- **Confluent's flat 0→100 curve makes sense.** Pool of 100 workers, 100µs of work per record → pool exactly saturated,
  no queuing. At 1000µs (workers can't drain fast enough) it falls to 67k.

## Allocation profile (`gc.alloc.rate.norm`, B / op)

Lower is better. JMH's GC profiler reports allocation per benchmark operation (= per record).

| Runtime                     | `workMicros=0` | `workMicros=100` | `workMicros=1000` |
| --------------------------- | -------------: | ---------------: | ----------------: |
| Confluent Parallel Consumer |             34 |               34 |                34 |
| Raw `KafkaConsumer` + VT    |             55 |              431 |               455 |
| Reactor Kafka               |            162 |               84 |               193 |
| KPipe                       |            924 |            1,484 |             1,468 |

### Observations

- **Confluent allocates least** (~34 B/op) and is consistent across the workload sweep — its pool of long-lived worker
  threads doesn't allocate much per record.
- **Raw** is second-leanest at `workMicros=0` (55 B/op). The big jump at 100/1000µs is the virtual-thread Runnable
  lambdas piling up while blocked.
- **Reactor** allocates moderately (84–193 B/op) — surprising given its throughput collapse, but the `Flux` machinery is
  not as allocation-heavy as you'd expect; the problem is scheduling, not bytes.
- **KPipe allocates most** (924–1,484 B/op) because of `Result<T>` wrappers, per-record builder hand-off, and a fresh
  virtual thread per record. **At the throughput shown above this allocation rate did not depress ops/sec** — the JVM's
  young gen absorbed it. On a tight heap or a workload sensitive to GC tail latency, watch this number.

Reactor's GC numbers at `workMicros=1000` look low (30 events / 170 ms) because the benchmark is barely running — at ~9
records/sec there's almost no allocation pressure. Don't read that as Reactor being well-behaved; the throughput tells
the real story.

## Comparison to 2026-05-16 run

Same hardware, same harness, same parameters. Headline ordering and qualitative findings reproduce. Cell-level numbers
shifted up to ~40%:

| Cell                              |     2026-05-16 |     2026-05-24 |      Δ |
| --------------------------------- | -------------: | -------------: | -----: |
| Raw `KafkaConsumer + VT` @ 1000µs |        482,639 |        417,920 | −13.4% |
| KPipe @ 1000µs                    |        430,526 |        483,061 | +12.2% |
| KPipe @ 0µs                       |        473,491 |        542,412 | +14.6% |
| Reactor @ 0µs                     |        256,648 |        150,081 | −41.5% |
| Confluent @ all cells             | 100,106…64,715 | 109,952…67,726 | +5–10% |

The Reactor at 0µs swing (−41%) is the biggest single change between runs. The qualitative "Reactor collapses under
blocking work" finding is unaffected; only the starting point of the collapse moved. For published comparisons, treat
single-laptop absolute numbers as one-sigma indicators of the qualitative ranking, not as point estimates.
